The story that started this

Chilli is still here because care was reachable in time.

That access was only possible because his community stepped in quickly enough to give him time, options, and a route to treatment. Many families who contacted the project did not get that window.

What happened to Chilli

Chilli has suspected MUO, but no definitive diagnosis because CSF sampling was too risky. He needed intensive care, MRI, anti-seizure medication, high-dose steroids, chemotherapy-style immune treatment, eye treatment, wound care, follow-up decisions, and careful management.

Why access mattered

Specialist care was available, but it had to be reachable quickly enough to make decisions possible.

What community support made possible

Chilli had time, options, and a route to treatment because his community stepped in quickly enough.

Why this became wider

Many families who contacted the project did not get that window. Their stories kept pointing to access, payment timing, insurance, referral costs, direct payment, and communication.

Where Chilli is now

Chilli is still here, still monitored, and still managed carefully.

Why this is not anti-vet

Chilli would not be here without vets, nurses, specialists, and hospital teams. The project is about care access, not blame.

Why the project keeps going

The project keeps collecting owner stories and Professional Insight so repeated barriers are harder to dismiss.

Why it matters

This became bigger than one dog.

The project exists because many people described care being available, but not reachable at the moment a decision had to be made.
