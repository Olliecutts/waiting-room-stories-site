About the project

Born from Chilli's story, built for something wider.

Chilli's illness is the reason this project started. After we asked for support with his care, we saw story after story from people who had faced the same impossible moment: care was available, but the money or payment system put it out of reach.

The Waiting Room Stories Project uses the platform built around Chilli to collect those experiences and turn them into evidence for change.

It is about emergency and specialist veterinary care broadly: ICU, imaging, surgery, referral, cancer and complex care, emergency stabilisation, ongoing treatment, and the payment barriers around them.

Why Chilli’s story started this

Chilli’s emergency showed how fast a family can run out of options.

When urgent specialist care, ICU, imaging, treatment, medication, and follow-up are needed, the costs can move faster than any normal family can prepare for. Insurance can help, but it can also run out, exclude what is needed, require upfront payment, or leave families waiting for reimbursement while decisions are being made in real time.

Chilli survived because thousands of people cared enough to help, share, donate, and keep his story moving. That support gave us something many families never get: time, options, and access to specialist care.

The Waiting Room Stories Project is our way of using the reach we have been given to show that Chilli’s case is not isolated.

We have a community that reaches millions of people. We want to use that reach to collect evidence, show the patterns, invite professional insight, and push for practical changes that help families before they reach the worst moment.

This is not about blaming vets. It is about the funding, insurance, payment, and access gaps around emergency and specialist care.

The heart of it

Care existed. Love existed. The money did not.

Families should not be left alone at the exact moment when treatment is possible but access depends on immediate money, insurance wording, or reimbursement timing.
