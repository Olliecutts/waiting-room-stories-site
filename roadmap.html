Project roadmap

The mission ahead

The Waiting Room Stories Project is turning painful owner experiences into public evidence for practical change.

We are collecting stories, showing patterns, and inviting professional insight from people across veterinary care, insurance, charity, payment, research, and animal welfare.

The goal is not to collect stories and leave them sitting in a spreadsheet.

The goal is to build a public evidence hub that shows what care was needed, what blocked access, whether insurance helped or failed, and what would have made a difference.

Every story helps make the patterns harder to ignore.

Current stage

Where we are now

We are in the evidence-building stage. The stories already shared are showing repeated patterns around upfront payment, insurance limits, treatment becoming unaffordable, reimbursement delays, and decisions being forced in very little time.

The next step is to keep growing the evidence, invite professional insight, and prepare the first stronger public briefing.

Professional input track

Why professional insight matters

Owner stories are only one part of the picture. We also need input from vets, vet nurses, reception teams, referral clinicians, practice managers, charities, insurers, payment providers, researchers, and people working across animal healthcare.

This is not an anti-vet project. Veterinary teams are often standing beside families during impossible decisions. We want to understand the funding, insurance, communication, and payment gaps around those moments.

Long-term vision

Long-term vision

The long-term vision is to build a trusted public evidence hub for emergency and specialist veterinary care access. A place where owner stories, public patterns, professional insight, and practical change goals can sit together.

A public evidence hub

Emergency and specialist veterinary care access patterns, shown clearly while protecting families.

Regular pattern updates

Clear updates that help people see where the same barriers keep appearing.

Professional and press briefings

Balanced summaries for veterinary teams, insurers, charities, researchers, journalists, and policy audiences.

Practical change partnerships

Work with organisations who can help improve payment routes, insurance clarity, emergency communication, and access to specialist care.

We are not an emergency fund, charity, or advice service. The aim is to collect evidence, show patterns, and push for practical change.
