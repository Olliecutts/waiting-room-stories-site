The Waiting Room Stories Project

Emergency and specialist vet care can exist and still be out of reach.

Care existed. Love existed. The money did not.

The Waiting Room Stories Project collects real owner stories from families who had to stop, delay, limit, or question care because of cost, insurance limits, upfront payment, reimbursement delays, or lack of fast support.

We are turning those stories into careful public evidence so we can push for clearer insurance, better payment options, and faster support when time matters.

What we are trying to change

Practical changes for the moment when time matters.

The project is focused on practical changes that could help families before they reach the worst moment.

Clearer pet insurance

Families should be able to understand limits, exclusions, referral wording, diagnostic cover, and reimbursement timing before an emergency happens.

Better emergency warnings

Policies should make it clearer how quickly ICU, imaging, hospitalisation, referral, surgery, or complex care can exceed cover.

More direct payment options

Where possible, insurers and veterinary providers should make direct-to-vet payment easier to understand and access.

Faster bridge options

Emergency payment routes need to work in hours, not weeks.

Better crisis information

Families need clear, kind explanations of likely costs, options, limits, and next steps while decisions are still possible.

Careful public evidence

The project shares patterns without exposing families, blaming veterinary teams, or pretending there is one simple fix.

What stories are already showing

The same themes are appearing in different kinds of care.

The latest project update shows the same themes appearing across different countries and different types of care.

Stories have involved ICU and hospitalisation, surgery, heart and breathing emergencies, cancer and complex care, diagnostic imaging, and specialist referral.

The clearest barriers so far are upfront payment, treatment becoming unaffordable, ICU costs, diagnostic imaging costs, and insurance not covering enough when families needed it.

This is early evidence, not a representative survey. But the pattern is already hard to ignore.

Professional insight

We need professional insight too

Owner stories are only one part of the picture. If you are a vet, vet nurse, receptionist, referral clinician, practice manager, charity worker, insurer, payment provider, researcher, or work anywhere in animal healthcare, we would value your perspective.

This is not an anti-vet project. Veterinary teams are often the people standing beside families during impossible decisions. We want to understand the funding, insurance, and payment gaps around those moments, not blame the people trying to help.

What we are asking

Help us show the patterns clearly.

What care was needed. What stopped it. Whether insurance helped or failed. What would have made a difference.

This is not about one condition or one treatment. The project covers emergency vet care, out-of-hours care, ICU, imaging, specialist referral, surgery, emergency stabilisation, ongoing treatment, cancer treatment, and complex medical care.
