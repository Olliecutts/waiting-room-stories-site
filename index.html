The Waiting Room Stories Project

Emergency and specialist vet care can exist and still be out of reach.

Care existed. Love existed. The money did not.

The Waiting Room Stories Project collects real owner stories from families who had to stop, delay, limit, or question care because of cost, insurance limits, upfront payment, reimbursement delays, or lack of fast support.

We are turning those stories into careful public evidence so we can push for clearer insurance, better payment options, and faster support when time matters.

What the latest update points to

  • Upfront payment and reimbursement timing can decide what is reachable.
  • ICU, imaging, surgery, referral, and complex care can move beyond cover quickly.
  • Insurance can exist and still fail families at the point they need care.

Why Chilli’s story matters

Chilli’s emergency showed how fast options can disappear.

The project began after Chilli’s care showed what many families already knew: love and care can both exist, while the payment system still leaves people with impossible choices.

This is not about blaming vets. It is about the funding, insurance, payment, and access gaps around emergency and specialist care.
