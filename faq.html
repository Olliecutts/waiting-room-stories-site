FAQ

Questions people ask

Short answers about what the project is, what it is not, and how stories are handled.

Is this blaming vets?

No. The project is not anti-vet. It is focused on the funding, payment, and insurance gap that can leave families and veterinary teams with very little room to move.

Can I share anonymously?

Yes. The story form asks how your story can be used. Public pages should not include names, contact details, pet names, exact locations, or identifying details.

What happens to my story?

Stories are reviewed for patterns such as what care was needed, what got in the way, whether insurance helped, and what might have made a difference.

Will you publish quotes?

Anonymous quotes may only be considered where permission allows and after human review. A story being shared does not mean it will automatically be quoted.

Can you help with current vet bills?

No. The project cannot provide urgent support, bill payment, funding, or professional advice. If your pet is in emergency care now, please speak directly with your vet or emergency hospital.

Are you a charity or fund?

No. The project is not a charity, fund, or donation route. It collects stories to show patterns and push for practical change.

Can vets or insurers contact you?

Yes. Vets, nurses, referral teams, insurers, charities, payment providers, and researchers can email waitingroom@kingchillithepug.com with the subject "Professional insight".

What are you trying to change?

Clearer pet insurance, better emergency warnings, more direct payment options, faster bridge options, better crisis information, and careful public evidence.

Why UK and US stories?

The project began with UK and US research because both systems show emergency and specialist care access problems, but in different ways. Other countries can still share stories, and country differences are kept visible in the patterns where useful.

How are patterns counted?

Stories are grouped into broad themes from the answers people choose and write. The public site shows grouped patterns only. Some details are reviewed privately so people stay protected, but every real story still contributes to the project.
