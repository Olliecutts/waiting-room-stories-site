Change

What we want to change

Practical fixes for the moment when time matters.

There is no single fix for emergency and specialist veterinary costs. But the stories being shared point toward practical changes that could reduce the gap between available care and reachable care.

Clearer pet insurance

Families should be able to understand limits, exclusions, referral wording, diagnostic cover, and reimbursement timing before an emergency happens.

Better emergency warnings

Policies should make it clearer how quickly ICU, imaging, hospitalisation, referral, surgery, or complex care can exceed cover.

More direct payment options

Where possible, insurers and veterinary providers should make direct-to-vet payment easier to understand and access.

Faster bridge options

Emergency payment routes need to work in hours, not weeks.

Better crisis information

Families need clear, kind explanations of likely costs, options, limits, and next steps while decisions are still possible.

Careful public evidence

The project shares patterns without exposing families, blaming veterinary teams, or pretending there is one simple fix.

These are not services currently offered by the project. They are the changes we are gathering evidence to push for.
